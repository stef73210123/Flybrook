Grown at dawn, served by lunch.

From May through October, our regenerative organic farm grows the vegetables, herbs and eggs that shape each day's menu. What's picked in Livingston Manor in the morning is on the table by lunch — at the farm itself, and roughly two hours later in Armonk.

It's the whole idea behind Flybrook: let the land set the menu, and keep the distance between the two as short as we can.

The name

Water, patience, and the land that feeds the kitchen.

Armonck

What the Lenape called this land — the fishing place between the hills. Our home in downtown Armonk takes its table from that same water and soil.

Livingston Manor

The storied home of American fly fishing, and where our farm sits. The brook, the hatch, the hand-tied fly — our mark honors all three.

Regenerative by hand

We farm to leave the ground better than we found it — cover crops, rotation, and a light touch, so the valley keeps feeding the kitchen for years to come.

Field and stream to fork — a new American table in Armonk and the Catskills.
